A lot of “funny about the months” jokes are based on wordplay, stereotypes, or what each month is known for. Here are a few classic examples: January — everyone suddenly becomes a gym expert for about 12 days. February — the shortest month somehow feels the longest if you’re waiting for payday. March — comes in like a lion, leaves everyone sneezing from pollen. April — the only month that officially encourages pranks on the 1st. May — people act like summer already started even

There isn’t one objectively “most important” month — it depends on what you mean: For businesses: often December because of holiday spending. For students: maybe September or January for new terms. For farmers: rainy or harvest seasons matter most. Personally: birthdays usually win. But scientifically, every month matters equally because the calendar is just a way humans divide time.
